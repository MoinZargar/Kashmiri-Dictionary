    History and Culture

    Kashmiri language belongs to the Indo-Aryan language family and has a rich history and culture. The earliest written records of the Kashmiri language date back to the 8th century CE, during the reign of King Lalitaditya. However, it wasn't until the 14th century that Kashmiri literature truly flourished. During the 14th and 15th centuries, the Sufi saints of Kashmir played a significant role in the development of Kashmiri language and literature. Their poetry and writings were instrumental in shaping the language, and their influence can still be seen in Kashmiri culture today. The Kashmiri language has a unique script, known as the Sharada script, which was used to write the language until the 20th century. Today, the language is primarily written in the Perso-Arabic script. Kashmiri literature includes a wide range of genres, including poetry, folktales, and religious texts. Some of the most famous Kashmiri poets include Lal Ded, Habba Khatoon,Mehjoor, Rasul Mir, G.N Doolwal (Janbaz Kishtwari) etc. In addition to literature, Kashmiri culture is also rich in music and dance. The traditional music of Kashmir, known as Sufiana music, is a blend of Persian and Indian classical music and is characterized by its haunting melodies and devotional lyrics. The traditional dance of Kashmir is known as the Rouf, which is performed by women during festivals and other celebrations. The Rouf is a graceful and elegant dance, characterized by its circular movements and intricate footwork. In conclusion, the Kashmiri language and culture have a rich and complex history, with influences from a variety of sources.
